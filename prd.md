## 2. Problem Statement
There has been a rapid acceleration in Artificial Intelligence over the past 3–4 years, particularly following the release of GPT-3. What was once experimental has now become widely accessible, with businesses, developers, and governments all beginning to explore how AI can be integrated into everyday systems.

At the same time, Zimbabwe has introduced data protection and cyber regulations, along with an emerging national Artificial Intelligence strategy. This creates a unique environment where there is both opportunity and constraint. On one hand, there is clear momentum toward adopting AI; on the other, there are growing responsibilities around how data is handled, stored, and used.

Despite this, there remains a gap. Many organizations and developers do not have the infrastructure, tools, or guidance needed to effectively build and deploy AI systems. The barrier is not just knowledge, but access to reliable, secure, and locally relevant infrastructure that aligns with regulatory expectations while still enabling innovation.

There is therefore a need for solutions that are tailored toward enabling industries, particularly in Sub-Saharan Africa, to adopt AI in a way that is both practical and scalable. The goal is not just adoption, but leapfrogging, allowing organizations to move directly into modern, AI-driven workflows without needing to build everything from scratch.

Sabhuku AI emerges in response to this gap. It is positioned as an Artificial Intelligence Platform as a Service (AI PaaS), providing a unified environment that brings together frontend experience, backend systems, data handling, and object storage into a single platform. Built using React and Django, it is designed to lower the barrier to entry while ensuring that systems are structured, secure, and aligned with current regulatory realities.

This matters now because AI is increasingly becoming a foundational layer, not just a feature. It is moving toward being treated as a utility, something that organizations depend on in the same way they depend on electricity or the internet. Without accessible platforms to support this shift, many will be left behind, not due to lack of interest, but due to lack of infrastructure

## 3. Goals & Success Metrics

### Goals
- Provide a platform for the exchange of ideas, learning resources, AI models, and training datasets
- Enable developers, organizations, and companies to easily host, deploy, and manage AI models through a simple, accessible interface
- Lower the barrier to entry for building and using AI by combining infrastructure, tools, and community in one place
- Support collaboration and knowledge sharing within and beyond Sub-Saharan Africa
- Offer a reliable and structured environment for developing AI systems that are secure and aligned with data regulations

### Success Metrics (KPIs)
Although these metrics are key to a successful product offering, "we are young and we have nothing to lose but everything to gain"- Steve Jobs. If we can 
Just learn, create networks that would be great. The actual KPIs below 
- Platform Adoption
    - Number of registered users (developers, organizations, companies)
    - Monthly active users (MAU)
    - User retention rate (30-day retention)
- AI Model Hosting & Deployment Usage
    - Number of AI models deployed on the platform
    - Number of active deployed models per month
    - Average deployment time from upload to production readiness
- Dataset & Knowledge Exchange Activity
    - Number of datasets uploaded and shared
    - Number of model or dataset downloads per month
    - Contribution rate (users contributing vs only consuming)
- Platform Engagement
    - Number of API calls or inference requests per day
    - Average session duration on platform
    - Feature usage distribution (uploading, training, deploying, sharing)
- Ease of Use / Accessibility
    - Time taken for first successful model deployment (new user onboarding metric)
    - User onboarding completion rate
    - Support tickets or friction points per user (lower is better)
- Reliability & Performance
    - API uptime percentage (these one the model hosting should have the best reliability 99.9)
    - Average inference latency (this tell us if we need to tune our hardware recommender system up or done)
    - Deployment success rate vs failure rate
- Ecosystem Growth
    - Number of organizations onboarded
    - Number of collaborative projects between users
    - Growth rate of active contributors (month-over-month)
